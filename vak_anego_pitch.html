What I built and why it matters

For the past five months I designed, built, and operated a complete trading platform for the Vintage Story TOPS server — solo, from scratch, while in my first year of university. It runs a custom ledger currency called EW (Exchange Weight) that solves three problems the base game cannot: barter friction, Rusty Gear inflation, and price opacity.

The system is not a prototype. It handles Google OAuth authentication, admin-approved trade queues, a player-to-player marketplace with peg-based pricing, a public Bank Dashboard showing EW circulation and wealth distribution, daily velocity tracking, and a metals-backed insurance mechanism — all built on a static frontend served via Cloudflare, a Worker proxy, and a Google Apps Script backend. Some players describe the game as nearly unplayable without it.

Vak's Store has already contributed to two rounds of optimization work in Vintage Story. Tyron visited the site. This is not a cold pitch — it is a follow-up to something that already caught the team's attention organically.

The problem worth solving at the base game level

Rusty Gears inflate because mob farms print them at zero economic cost. Barter is slow, trust-dependent, and geographically punishing on a large server. New players have no price reference and are routinely exploited by veterans. These problems exist on every TOPS-style server and will keep existing until something structural changes.

Vintage Story's design philosophy — everything costs real effort, almost everything has value — is exactly what makes a well-designed economy possible here in a way it simply isn't in Minecraft or most MMOs. The game deserves an economy layer worthy of that design.

What exists today (Vak's Store)
  • Manual admin-approved trades
  • Single server (TOPS)
  • Community-run, one operator
  • Dependent on Vak's availability
  • Data collected but not natively integrated
What it could become (in-game)
  • Automated, always-on, no admin bottleneck
  • Multi-server, each economy independent
  • Native to the game, not a third-party tool
  • Public economy data visible to all players
  • Real research environment, open to academia

The vision: Vintage Story as a living economics laboratory

"Imagine every Vintage Story server running the same economic framework — each one a small, isolated economy with its own population, culture, and dynamics. All of it public. Players learn supply and demand by living it. Researchers study small-scale market behavior without controlled experiments. Developers see exactly how their design decisions ripple through real economies."

This is not speculative. The architecture already exists in miniature on TOPS. The EW model — issuance tied to real item supply, dual-sink destruction, algorithmic price adjustment, and full public observability — is documented, tested across multiple wipe cycles, and proven to be more stable than Rusty Gears in every measurable dimension.

Extending it to a native, multi-server system would give Vintage Story something no other survival game has: a transparent, research-grade economy that teaches real economic principles through play, with no real money at stake.
